Background

There are two articles about the same event of each case, called "recalled story", and "retold story".
• recalled : 
A memorable or salient story that the author experienced in the past 6 months.
• retold : 
After 2-3 months, the authors are asked to re-tell the events, providing them with the summary of their story as a prompt.

Annotation Description

There are two parts in this labeling work: Span Labeling and Relation Tagging
Outline
1. Click 'View Instructions' and read the descriptions on this page.
2. According to the detail rule below, start to label the spans.
3. Click "Start Relation Tagging" and start tagging the relations.
    - There would pop up a floating box that allows you to tag the relation via that.
    - The tagging result would be listed below.
Detail
1. Label the spans : 
Select the spans of life events of the author in recalled and retold story, which are related to the event of the summary, i.e. Any 5W(When, Who, Where, What, Why) about the event.
WHAT is Span:
The tightest span in the sentence that related to the life event, i.e. the span is a sub-sequence of a sentence and there may be multiple spans in one sentence. e.g.
I can't exactly remember the day but I believe it was either April or May or maybe June.-> Only select the span that relate to the life event.
When we walked in there was the insect/reptile section of the zoo so we saw many exotic looking spiders, snakes, scorpions etc.-> There are two life events in one sentence.
We walked in insect/reptile section of the zoo and saw many exotic looking spiders, snakes, scorpions etc.-> There are two life events in one sentence, but the subject only appear once. In this case, you can just select the whole sentence. However, in the relation tagging part(detail listed below), you should consider that there are two different life event and tagging to different relations.
relation recalled retold
match We walked in insect/reptile section of the zoo and saw many exotic looking spiders, snakes, scorpions etc. We entered the insction
match We walked in insect/reptile section of the zoo and saw many exotic looking spiders, snakes, scorpions etc. We saw many kinds of spiders

WHAT is Life Events:
Life event means the sentence contains personal experiences about “Who did What to Whom Where When and How.”
WHAT kinds of life events need to be labeled:
1. 
The events related to personal experiences, e.g.
I went to Salt Lake City on business with Mr. Wang.
I think we also saw kangaroos but I can't remember.-> The event may not exactly happened so that don't select these spans.
2. 
About someone's personal information, e.g.
I skipped fourth grade.
I live in Taiwan.
3. 
5W1H(When, Who, Where, What, Why, How) relate to the event of the storys.
(Summary: Me and my girlfriend went to the zoo on a hot day. We saw many animals like giraffes, gorillas, elephants, snakes, tigers, lions etc..)
it was the middle of July.-> It relates to the event.
it was a pretty hot day.-> It only describes the environment but doesn't relates to the event so that don't select these spans.
4. 
The sentences which express someone's thoughts or feelings imply that something happened, otherwise, don't select the span, e.g.
These cookies taste delicious. -> Imply that the author ate the cookies so that please select this span.
I like Chinese food -> Only express personal preference so that don't select these spans
I think you are cute. -> Only express someone's thoughts so that don't select these spans.
I’m tired of going to school. -> Not imply any life event so that don't select these spans.
It's a hot day. -> Not imply any life event so that don't select these spans.
5. 
In most cases, the below patterns won't be selected.
I feel...
I like...
2. Tag the relations : 
Tag the relations between the spans in the recalled and retold stories with three types, including "Match", "Conflict" and "Add".
Match : 
The spans from both recalled story and retold story describe the same thing, and there are no conflicts between each other.
Notice: No matter which span describe in better detail, we only concern that the spans are describing the same life event.
Take the green box of below picture for example, both spans from recalled and retold storys describe the life event about they found the gorillas so that we tagged the relation of these spans as "match".
Conflict : 
In contrast to "Match", "Conflict" means the events described in the spans from both recalled story and retold storys are inconsistent.
Take the red box of below picture for example, the month of the same life event from recalled and retold story are different, so that we tagged the relation of this ids pair as "conflict".
Added : 
If the life event only appeared in the retold story, please tag the spans with "Added". In this case, only the spans in the retold story need to be tagged.
Take the blue box of below picture for example, the time information of the life event only appeared in the retold story, so that we tagged the relation as "added".
Note that:
1 . 
Each span represents a life event or part of description of life event.
2 . 
The mapping between the spans in the recalled and retold stories may be one-to-one, one-to-many, many-to-one, or many-to-many to denote the relation types of "Match" and "Conflict".
For example,
  - one-to-many means one span in the recalled may be mapped to multiple spans in the retold story.
  - Many-to-one means multiple spans in the recalled may be mapped to one span in the retold story.
3 . 
All labeled spans of retold story should be tagged at least one relation type.
4 . 
Because the time gap between recalled story and retold story is greater than half year, the relative time duration may be different, but relation might be "Match". (Row1: relative time description; Row2: absolute time description)

match I had worked for it for 13 years. I had worked forit for 14 years.
conflict We go the zoo in April. It's happened in July.
