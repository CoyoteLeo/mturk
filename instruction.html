Background

There are two articles about the same event of each case, called "recalled story", and "retold story".
• recalled : 
A memorable or salient story that the author experienced in the past 6 months.
• retold : 
After 2-3 months, the authors are asked to re-tell the events, providing them with the summary of their story as a prompt.

Annotation Description

There are two parts in this lebeling work: Span Labeling & Relation tagging
Outline
1. Click 'View Instructions' and read the describtions in this page.
2. According to the detail rule below, start to label the spans.
3. Click "Start Relation Tagging" and start tagging the relations.
    - There would pop up a floating box which allow you to tag the relation via that.
    - The tagging result would be listed in the below.
Detail
1. Label the spans : 
Select the spans of life events of the author in recalled and retold story, which are related to the event of the summary, i.e. Any 5W(When, Who, Where, What, Why) about the event.
WHAT is life events:
Life event means the sentence contains personal experiences about “Who did What to Whom Where When and How.”
WHAT kinds of life events need to be labeled:
1. 
The events related to personal experiences, e.g.
I went to Salt Lake City on business with Mr. Wang.
2. 
About someone's personal information, e.g.
I skipped fourth grade.
I live in Taiwan.
3. 
5W1H(When, Who, Where, What, Why, How) relate to the event of the storys.
(Summary: Me and my girlfriend went to the zoo on a hot day. We saw many animals like giraffes, gorillas, elephants, snakes, tigers, lions etc..)
it was the middle of July.-> It relates to the event.
4. 
The sentences which express someone's thoughts or feelings implies that something happened, otherwise, don't select the span, e.g.
These cookies taste delicious. -> Imply that the author ate the cookies so that please select this span.
I like Chinese food -> Only express personal preference and not imply any life event so that don't select these spans
I’m tired of going to school. -> Not imply any life event so that don't select these spans.
It's a hot day. -> Not imply any life event so that don't select these spans.
5. 
In most cases, the below patterns won't be selected.
I feel...
I like...
I think...
2. Tag the relations : 
Tag the relations between the spans in the recalled and retold stories with three types, including "Match", "Conflict" and "Add".
Match : 
The spans from both recalled story and retold story describe the same thing, and there are no conflicts between each other.
Notice: No matter which span describe in better detail, we only concern that the spans are describing the same life event.
Take the green box of below picture for example, both spans from recalled and retold story describe the life event about they found the gorillas, so that we tagged the relation of these spans as "match".
Conflict : 
In contrast to "Match", "Conflict" means the events described in the spans from both recalled story and retold story are inconsistent.
Take the red box of below picture for example, the month of the date of same life event is totally different, so that we tagged the relation of this ids pair as "conflict".
Added : 
If the life event only appeared in the retold story, please tag the spans with "Added". In this case, only the spans in the retold story need to be tagged.
Take the blue box of below picture for example, the time information of the life event only appeared in the retold story, so that we tagged the relation as "added".
Note that:
1 . 
The mapping between the spans in the recalled and retold stories may be one-to-one, one-to-many, many-to-one, or many-to-many to denote the relation types of "Match" and "Conflict".
For example,
  - one-to-many means one span in the recalled may be mapped to multiple spans in the retold story.
  - Many-to-one means multiple spans in the recalled may be mapped to one span in the retold story.
2 . 
All labeled spans of retold story should be tagged at least one relation type.
